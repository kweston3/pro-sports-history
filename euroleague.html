The History of the EuroLeague

Dive deep into the history of the EuroLeague, exploring how the league began and evolved over time, from its early days to its current status as a premier European basketball competition.

Founded in 1958 as the European Champions Cup, the EuroLeague began as a competition for the top teams in European basketball. The league evolved over the decades, becoming the official EuroLeague in 2000, with a format and structure designed to enhance the level of competition and visibility.

The 2000s saw the EuroLeague grow in stature, driven by the participation of top clubs from across Europe and the emergence of standout players. Teams like Real Madrid, CSKA Moscow, and Panathinaikos became dominant forces, showcasing the high level of basketball across the continent.

Today, the EuroLeague stands as a leading basketball competition in Europe, featuring elite teams and top international talent. Its history is marked by significant milestones, memorable games, and a commitment to promoting and advancing the sport of basketball across Europe.
